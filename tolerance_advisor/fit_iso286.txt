# ISO 286 – Geometrical Product Specifications: Limits and Fits

ISO 286 is the international standard that defines how tightly or loosely two mating parts should fit together.

---

## What the standard gives you

ISO 286 provides a complete, codified system built around a few core concepts:

**Tolerance grades (IT grades)** — There are 20 standard tolerance grades, from IT01 (extremely precise) up to IT18 (very coarse). 

**Fundamental deviations** — These define the *position* of the tolerance zone relative to the nominal size. 
They are identified by letters: lowercase for shafts (a–z) and uppercase for holes (A–Z). 
The deviation tells you how far the tolerance zone is offset from the zero line (nominal dimension).

**Fit system** — Combining a fundamental deviation with a tolerance grade gives you a complete tolerance designation, for example `H7` for a hole or `k6` for a shaft. Pairing them (e.g. `H7/k6`) defines the fit between two mating parts.

**Three categories of fit:**
- **Clearance fit** — the shaft is always smaller than the hole (e.g. `H7/f7`). Used for sliding or rotating parts.
- **Interference fit** — the shaft is always larger than the hole (e.g. `H7/p6`). Used for press fits and force-transmission joints.
- **Transition fit** — can result in either slight clearance or slight interference (e.g. `H7/k6`). Used for accurate location with easy assembly.

---

## How it is used in practice

Engineers apply ISO 286 directly on engineering drawings. A typical callout looks like this on a drawing:

> ⌀ 50 H7 / g6

This tells the machinist: nominal diameter is 50 mm, the hole shall be machined to H7 tolerance, the shaft to g6 tolerance, 
and the resulting fit is a clearance (running/sliding) fit.

The standard is used together with **ISO 286-1** (which tabulates the numerical values of tolerance grades and deviations) and 
**ISO 286-2** (which provides tables of preferred fits for direct use). 

---

## When to use it

ISO 286 is the right tool whenever you have **mating cylindrical features** that need to be manufactured separately and assembled together. 
Typical situations:

- **Bearings in housings** — bearing outer ring in a housing bore, or inner ring on a shaft, almost always specified with ISO 286 fits.
- **Shafts in bores** — gearboxes, pumps, motors, any rotating machinery.
- **Locating pins and bushings** — where precise positioning is required.
- **Press-fit joints** — transmitting torque or axial force without fasteners.
- **Sliding or reciprocating components** — pistons, guide rods, spigots.

As a general rule: if two parts are made by different people or processes and must assemble reliably and interchangeably, 
ISO 286 provides the framework to make that happen without individual fitting or measurement at assembly.

---

## Key practical takeaways

- The **H/h system** is the baseline: `H` means the lower deviation of the hole is zero (hole-basis system), 
`h` means the upper deviation of the shaft is zero (shaft-basis system). 
The hole-basis system (`H` holes) is strongly preferred in practice because holes are harder to adjust than shafts.
- **Prefer standard fits** from ISO 286-2 whenever possible — they are well-documented, widely understood by machinists, and tooling is optimized for them.
- Tighter IT grades cost significantly more to produce. Always select the *coarsest* grade that meets your functional requirement.
- The standard applies strictly to **plain (non-threaded, non-geared) cylindrical features**. 
Threads, gears, and splines have their own separate ISO standards.




Here is a practical reference of the most widely used fits in the **hole-basis system (H holes)**:

| Fit | Type | Typical application |
|---|---|---|
| H11/c11 | Loose clearance | Very loose, dirty or wide temperature range |
| H9/d9 | Free running | Widely toleranced rotating parts, low precision |
| H8/f7 | Close running | General running fits, grease or oil lubricated |
| H7/g6 | Sliding | Accurate location, slow movement or sliding |
| H7/h6 | Close sliding | Precise location, no intentional play |
| H7/k6 | Transition | Accurate location, slight interference possible |
| H7/n6 | Transition | More positive location, light press needed |
| H7/p6 | Light interference | Press fit, permanent but can be disassembled |
| H7/s6 | Medium interference | Drive fits, high force transmission |
| H7/u6 | Heavy interference | Permanent joints, high torque or shock loads |
